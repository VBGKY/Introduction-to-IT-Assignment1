The 70s: A Glance at the Arcade Revolution

The 70s was the decade that introduced the world to arcade games. The games introduced in the 70s were very simple but would go on to provide the foundation for the arcade game technology we all know and love today. Some notable games included:

  • "Computer Space" (1971)
  • "Galaxy Game" (1971)
  • "Pong" (1972)
  • "Space Invaders" (1978)
  • "Computer Space" (1971)

The 80s: Arcade Glory Days

The 80s was where arcade games made some small advancements, brining colour and more functionality to the scene. There were some very recognisable games released in this era including:

  • "Pac-Man" (1980)
  • "Donkey Kong" (1981)
  • "Street Fighter" (1987)
  • "Defender" (1981)
  • "Galaga" (1981)

The 90s: Technological Evolution

The 90s was the decade that really stepped things up, bringing 3D graphics and introducing rhythm games such as ‘Dance Dance Revolution’, which brought physical aspects to the arcade world. Some notable games included:

  • "Mortal Kombat" (1992)
  • "Tekken" (1994)
  • "Dance Dance Revolution" (1998)
  • "Time Crisis" (1995)
  • "House of the Dead (1996)

The 2000s: Evolution and Diversity

The 2000s was the era where gaming started to shift to console and mobile gaming. As most people preferred the idea of playing from the comfort of their home, developers such as Sega and Nintendo started to shift their focus to console gaming. However, there were still some notable games such as:

  • "Dance Dance Revolution Max (2001)"
  • "DDR Extreme" (2002)
  • "Time Crsis 3 (2002)
  • "The House of the Dead 3 (2002)
  • "Virtua Fighter 4 (2001)
