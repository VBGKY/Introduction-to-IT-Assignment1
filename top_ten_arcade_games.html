Number 1:Pac-Man

Pac-Man is one of the most iconic and well known arcade games in history. Since its release in the United States in 1980, approximentally 400,000 Units have been sold. Due to the boom in popularity, the revenue generated by 1985 was a heafty $3.5 billion USD.
For a more information and an indepth look into Pac-Man, head to the Pac-Man page. of our website!

Number 2:Space Invaders

Space Invaders was developed by Taito and released in 1979. This arcade game has be credited for a the era of gaming known as the 'Golden Age of Arcades'. With 360,000 cabinets sold and a revenue of $2.7 billion USD generated by 1982, this iconic shooting style arcade game deserves second place.

Number 3:Street Fighter II/Champion Edition

Street Fighter II/Champion Edition (released in 1992) is the sequal to the popular Arcade Game Street Fighter (released in 1987 ), with the champion edition specifically being one of various updates for Street Fighter II. This version of the well known 90's combat game has sold 200,000 units and produced $2.3 billion by 1995.

Number 4:Ms Pacman

This varient of the original and popular Pacman arcade game was developed by Bally Midway and released in 1982. The development of this game to include a female version of Pac-Man was due to how popular the original Pac-Man game was amongst female gamers. This variation also proved popular in its own right. Generating $1.2 billion of revenue by 1987 and selling 125,00 arcade units.

Number 5: NBA Jam

This basketball arcade game was developed by Midway and released in 1993. Popularity for this game has be credited to the NBA licensing, which allowed for the game to feature various well known NBA athletes. The adoration of this game is reflected in the 20,000 machines sold and the $1.1 billon of revenue by 1994

Number 6: Defender

Defender was a space themed sideways-scrolling shooter style arcade game developed by Williams and released in 1981. This form of arcade game was the first of its kind at the time, and proved extremly popular. By 1991 the arcade game had managed to generate $1 billion in revenue and 60,000 arcade units were sold.

Number 7: Asteroids

This classic interstellar arcade game was released in 1979 and developed by famous game developers Atari. The gamplay featured in Asteroids was highly developed for its time, as the player was requqired to naviagte and mitgate incomming enimies from all sides. This is unlike other games at the time such as the previous mentioned space invaiders. 100,000 arcade cabinets were sold and by 1991 the game has produced $8 million in revenue.

Number 8: Mortal Kombat II

This seuqal to Mortal Kombat was developed by Midway and released in 1993. With significant improvements, new features and new moves added to the gamplay, (such as the crouching punch) this version of Mortal Kombat was unsurprisingly more popular than its predassesor. 27,000 units of the game were sold and by 2002, it had produced $6 millon in revenue.

Number 9: Mortal Kombat

This 1v1 combat game was released in 1992 by Midway. This game unlike others featured realistic digitised characters. With 24,000 units sold and generating a revenue of $5.7 million, this arcade game has earnt its place in the top 10!

Number 10: Donkey kong

This platforming game released by gaming developer giants Nintendo in 1981. This game featured various characters who would become house-hold names such as Jumpman and Pauline who would become Mario and Princess Peach respectivly. This game proved itself popular. Generating $2.8 million by 1982 and selling 132,00 units.
